# Chronicle Blurbs

Reusable short-form copy for release notes, repo copy, emails, and demo materials.

## One Sentence

Chronicle is an accessibility-first document recovery tool for hard real-world material, especially degraded scans, books, comics, manga, graphic novels, archives, complex layouts, and review-heavy workflows.

## Short Product Blurb

Chronicle is built for difficult documents. It helps recover accessible, review-ready output from degraded scans, long-form books, comics, manga, graphic novels, archival material, newspapers, forms, reports, and mixed-format batches. Instead of treating every file the same way, Chronicle gives users specialist presets, queue controls, clear progress, and fidelity-first recovery for hard real-world material.

## Short Demo Intro

Chronicle is most useful when the document is messy, damaged, or structurally awkward. It is designed for people who need better results on difficult scans, not just faster results on easy files. That makes it a strong fit for accessibility review, archival recovery, transcription-heavy work, and long documents that need careful reruns.

## Public-Repo Intro

Chronicle is an accessibility-first, visually intelligent document recovery tool for difficult real-world material. It is especially strong on degraded scans, long-form books, comics, manga, graphic novels, archival records, newspapers, legal/policy documents, forms, and mixed-format queues where preserving reading order, usable structure, and review workflow matters more than raw one-click speed.

## Comics Preset Blurb

Chronicle includes a dedicated `Comics / Manga / Graphic Novels` preset for accessible panel-by-panel reading output. It preserves visible panel order, speech and thought balloons, captions, signs, visible `SFX`, meaningful art descriptions, textless action panels, and right-to-left manga flow when the page visibly supports it.

## Email / Pitch Paragraph

Chronicle is designed for the kinds of documents people describe as "the scan is awful" or "the OCR is almost right but keeps breaking the text." It focuses on hard-document recovery: preserving paragraph continuity, cleaning repeated page furniture, rebuilding usable structure, and producing outputs that are easier to review in HTML or Word. Chronicle works best when a human operator needs to inspect, tune, and rerun difficult material rather than rely on a generic conversion pass. It is designed to reduce manual cleanup, not eliminate review.
