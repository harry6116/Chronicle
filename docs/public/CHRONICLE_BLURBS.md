# Chronicle Blurbs

Reusable short-form copy for websites, release notes, emails, and demo materials.

## One Sentence

Chronicle is an accessibility-first document recovery tool for hard real-world material, especially degraded scans, books, archives, complex layouts, and review-heavy workflows.

## Short Homepage Blurb

Chronicle is built for difficult documents. It helps recover accessible, review-ready outputs from degraded scans, long-form books, archival material, newspapers, forms, reports, and mixed-format batches. Instead of treating every file as the same problem, Chronicle gives users specialist presets, automatic engine routing with manual override when needed, queue controls, clear progress, and fidelity-first recovery for hard real-world material.

## Short Demo Intro

Chronicle is most useful when the document is messy, damaged, or structurally awkward. It is designed for people who need better results on difficult scans, not just faster results on easy files. That makes it a strong fit for accessibility review, archival recovery, transcription-heavy work, and long documents that need careful reruns instead of one blind pass.

## Public-Repo Intro

Chronicle is an accessibility-first, visually intelligent document recovery tool for difficult real-world material. It is especially strong on degraded scans, long-form books, archival records, newspapers, legal/policy documents, forms, and mixed-format queues where preserving reading order, usable structure, and review workflow matters more than raw one-click speed.

## Email / Pitch Paragraph

Chronicle is designed for the kinds of documents that are usually described as "the scan is awful" or "the OCR is almost right but keeps breaking the text." It focuses on hard-document recovery: preserving paragraph continuity, cleaning repeated page furniture, rebuilding usable structure, and producing outputs that are easier to review in HTML or Word. Chronicle works especially well when a human operator needs to inspect, tune, and rerun difficult material rather than rely on a single generic conversion pass, and it is designed to reduce manual cleanup rather than eliminate human review. Automatic engine routing keeps easier jobs moving faster, while manual overrides and collision controls leave the operator in charge when a rerun needs a different path.
