# Chronicle One-Page Overview

## What Chronicle Is

Chronicle is an accessibility-first document recovery tool for difficult real-world material.

It is designed to recover readable, reviewable, structurally useful output from:

- degraded scans
- long-form books and memoirs
- archival and historical records
- newspapers and multi-column layouts
- comics, manga, graphic novels, and comic strips
- legal and policy documents
- forms, reports, and mixed-format batches

Chronicle is built to help users reach review-ready output faster on hard documents, while still assuming human review before final use.

## What Makes Chronicle Useful

Chronicle is most valuable when the document is hard.

Its strength is not simply extracting text. Its strength is recovering a usable reading experience while preserving fidelity to the source.

That includes:

- better paragraph continuity on wrapped or damaged scans
- panel-by-panel recovery for comics, manga, and graphic novels
- accessible reading scripts for speech balloons, captions, visible SFX, and textless action panels
- suppression of repeated page furniture when it harms reading flow
- optional preservation of printed page references for transcription and source tracking
- specialist document presets for different source classes
- structured outputs that are easier to review in HTML or Word
- transparent progress, queue handling, and rerun-friendly workflows

## Why People Choose Chronicle

### Built for difficult recovery work

Chronicle is tuned for the ugly cases:

- pages with damage, noise, or bad OCR behavior
- books where prose continuity matters
- archival material with folios, marginal clutter, or repeated headers
- large queues where different source types need different treatment

### Gives the operator control

Chronicle is designed for users who need to inspect, adjust, and rerun.

Key workflow strengths include:

- queue-first operation
- document presets
- seamless merge
- page and slide scope controls
- automatic engine routing with manual override when needed
- output collision controls for skip, overwrite, or auto-number behavior
- readable logs and visible in-progress temp outputs

### Accessibility-first by design

Chronicle aims to produce outputs that are more usable with assistive technology, not just raw extracted text.

That includes:

- semantic HTML
- Word-friendly structured output
- accessibility-aware reading order
- options that balance smooth reading against transcription/reference needs

## What Users Should Expect

Chronicle prioritizes quality, fidelity, and navigable structure over raw speed on difficult material.

- easy files may finish quickly
- difficult books, comics, newspapers, and degraded PDFs may take much longer
- that extra time is usually spent preserving reading order, usable structure, and better continuity on hard scans
- Chronicle can now start easier PDFs on the faster Gemini path and escalate only the hard pages or hard documents when automatic routing is enabled

Chronicle is a strong fit for:

- accessibility reviewers
- archivists
- transcription-heavy projects
- long difficult book scans
- comics, manga, graphic novels, and comic strips
- mixed-format recovery workflows

## Short Summary

Chronicle is for people who need better results on ugly documents, not just faster results on easy ones.
